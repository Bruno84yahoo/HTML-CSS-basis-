Let's Talk About Crow

Crows are smart and playful

Crows are considered intelligent animals. It is said that the raven with wolves can form a pact. If a crow saw a wounded animal, it would look for a wolf and attract his attention. After the wolf is done with the prey, the crow will continue to eat it. Ravens are also known to use "tools"; with chopsticks they pick up the food from the cavities and with stones they break the nuts. The crow can mimic the sounds of its environment, such as the human voice.

The raven is significantly larger than the black raven and the tower. The length of the head-tail is 65 centimeters, the wingspan of 1.20 meters and the average weight is 1250 grams. The beak is relatively heavier and slightly curved. In flight, the bird can be recognized by the relatively larger head profile and wider, flared, diamond-shaped tail. It also flies higher than the aforementioned species and with a slower wing flap, it floats regularly for a while, while an air line flies more intentionally somewhere. The noise is lower than with crows.
